Suresh Yadav drove from Lucknow to Mumbai last week — 1,420 km, three days, a 16-tonne load of textiles. He saved ₹2,000 in diesel. Here's the breakdown.

## The route the AI suggested

Google would have sent him via Indore. TranZfort's route AI factored in current diesel prices at stations along the way, weight-adjusted fuel curves for his truck model, and a known traffic pattern around Bhopal that adds 40 minutes during evening hours.

- Distance: 1,420 km (vs 1,395 — yes, longer)
- Diesel consumed: 178 L (vs 195 L on default route)
- Total fuel cost: ₹16,020 (vs ₹18,055)
- Time: 38 hours (vs 39 hours)

> **Tip: Longer is sometimes cheaper**
> Counter-intuitive but real: a 25 km longer route with two cheaper fuel stops and gentler gradients beat the shortest path on cost.

## What Suresh said

> "Pehle main sochta tha shortest route hi best hai. Ab samajh aaya ki 'best' alag cheez hai."
> — Suresh Yadav

₹2,000 isn't a fortune. But Suresh runs this route eight times a month. That's ₹16,000 a month — roughly his EMI. The AI doesn't have to be magical. It just has to be right, consistently.
